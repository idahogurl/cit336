Collaboration

Collaboration - the ability to work together to accomplish a task, goal or outcome - is a skill that is consistently mentioned by employers as a requirement for new and seasoned employees. In this course you will be requried to work with others on a weekly basis using both the discussion board and team meetings. Each individual is responsible for her or his own work, but interacting, supporting, assisting and collaborating are desirable and required in this class.

Work Team

During week 2 you will organize yourselves into teams of 3 or 4. In an online environment it is strongly suggested that you work with others in or near your own timezone whenever possible. The team will become your primary source for feedback and help. You are required to meet together each week to discuss the content of the week, assessments, insights and help one another with these things. These team meetings will be arranged for a location (face-to-face, Skype, Google Hangouts, etc...) and time that is acceptable to those in the team. Your attendance at and participation in the weekly meeting will be graded.

Each week you are required to complete a report. One question asks about your discussion board participation. As long as you participated (2 or more posts) on Slack in the #general or #tech-learning channel, then you can count that as discussion board participation.

Tip: Install the iOS or Android version on your smartphone in addition to the desktop versions. You'll find it far easier to earn your participation points by being more connected.

Tips

Here are some useful tips in using our Slack workspace.
Note: Our workspace has several plugins installed that other Slack workspaces may not. A double asterisk indicates it is a plugin specific command.

  • Use @here in your message to notify everyone online of your message.
  • Type @ and then start typing to select a person to notify them of your message.
