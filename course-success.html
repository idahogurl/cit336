Class Expectations

I want to start you off on the right foot. With the following information, I hope you will have a clear understanding of what to expect and what it takes to succeed in this class. I don't want to scare anyone off, but I want you all to honestly evaluate if you meet the expectations.

You will find programming courses are NOT your typical 3 credit classes. There is lots of material to cover and only 14 weeks to do so. You must expect to put in at least 9-15 hours per week. Please take a moment and consider your future in this course.

As the syllabus states:

You must be self-motivated to succeed in this course. There are expectations of constant learning and application — be anxiously engaged! Previous students have commented that you can do well in this course only if you stay caught up and work to grasp the concepts and apply them. Take their advice!

Practice what you learn! Pull up your editor and try out things. Experiment! I put a link to a PHP sandbox below where you can try out PHP code.

Getting Help

It is NOT a matter of IF, rather WHEN you will need help. Act quickly when you need help. When you start struggling, even the tiniest bit, go get help! Do NOT put it off! There are several resources to get help:

  • Prayer
  • Classmates
  • Schedule an online tutor. Click online tutor to the left to schedule a tutoring session. DO NOT underestimate how much just one tutoring session will help you.
  • Internet & Programming Books: You MUST learn how use the Internet and books as a resource. It is part of being a programmer! I am constantly looking up resources, and reading API and reference material while working. There are a MASSIVE number of programming sites, books, and documentation for PHP. Your textbook is not a comprehensive PHP guide.
  • I am available via e-mail and Slack all day. I prefer Slack to email. Usually get a response immediately with Slack. I am also available by appointment. You can sign up for an available time slot by going to https://rvest.youcanbook.me
