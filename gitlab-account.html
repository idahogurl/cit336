Git Version Control

Introduction

We will be using Git version control to submit assignments. You will make a branch of your master repository, commit to that branch, and then create a merge request for me to review. If this sounds Greek to you, don't worry, I will cover this in video tutorials. Those who had CIT 230 may already be familiar with Git version control.

You will still be required to submit your assignment. Instead of a ZIP file of your code, you will provide a document or text file with the link to your video recording. The submission date of that file will be used to determine whether the assignment was late.

Setup GitLab Account and Repository

Create an Account

  1. Open a web browser and type "gitlab.com."
  2. Once the GitLab website loads go to the upper-right corner, click "Register."
  3. Finish the remainder of the form as directed and click "Register."
  4. You will receive a verification email shortly from GitLab. If you do not receive one, check your Spam or Junk folder.
  5. Open the email and follow the directions given.

Create a Repository

  1. Log into your GitLab account. If you are already logged go to the upper-left corner and click the GitLab logo. This will take you to your project list.
  2. When the project list loads, go to the upper-right corner, click the green "New Project" button.
  3. Enter "acme" as the project name. The remaining settings we will leave as is. Make note the default selection for "Visibility" is "Private." You must explicitly grant permission to access your project.
  4. Click the green "Create project" button located at the bottom of the form.

Add Me as a Collaborator

  1. Log into your GitLab account. If you are already logged go to the upper left corner and click the GitLab logo. This will take you to your project list.
  2. When the project list loads click on your "acme" project.
  3. Locate the sidebar on the left and click "Settings". It is the last item in the sidebar.
  4. Once loaded click "Members" underneath it.
  5. In the right panel, click in the textbox below "Select members to invite."
  6. Type "idahogurl" or "Rebecca Vest" to find my account.
  7. Once the search completes, select "Rebecca Vest" from the result.
  8. Under "Choose a role permission," select "Developer."
  9. Click "Add to project."
